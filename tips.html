Good things

Purchases, habits, and miscellaneous tricks you might want to adopt.

Other people's writings in this vein: 100 ways to live better (Jacob Falkovich), 100 tips for a better life (Ideopunk), SSC product recommendations (2014, 2015), Stuff I Own and Use (Nintil), Favorite Things (Mark Xu)

Things to Buy

  • If you bake at home, get some disposable shower caps: they're an extremely cheap reusable way to cover many sizes of bowl and see how the dough's doing inside. I also like covering sheet pans with these bags.
  • A high-quality cordless vacuum becomes convenient enough to use that you start actually using it even when guests aren't coming over - it's one of the household items I'm most happy to have shelled out extra money for. (We have a Dyson V10 Cyclone Absolute.)
  • Extremely comfortable furniture, like the maximally-soft-and-cozy Home Depot Ethereal Shag Rug (eternal glory to Duncan Sabien for discovering this one) or very soft blankets (not making claims to optimality for this one, it's just pretty cheap and good).

    I also love this chair frame from World Market combined with this cushion (it just wraps you up like a burrito), but it takes up a lot of floorspace and is extremely inconvenient to order because availability is spotty and they refuse to deliver.
  • Dandelion Chocolate makes interesting and reliably tasty high-quality chocolate bars that taste shockingly different from ordinary chocolate or each other. Makes a good gift choice, too.
  • A small bottle of balsamic glaze lasts forever and is a great way to brighten up foods for which a little extra acidity and sweetness would help. I drizzle it over ice cream, add as a garnish on pasta dishes, and apply to pizzas that don't have enough tomato sauce to bring the necessary sweetness + acidity.
  • I'm not usually one for cute bodycare products, but shampoo bars last a shockingly long time, smell nice, and don't require me to fumble with the plastic top of a precarious bottle. (They lather way more than you'd naively expect.) I like these two from Lush but I haven't sampled very many.

Things to Do

  • Download an adblocker.
  • If you don't like it when apps coerce your perfectly good ASCII emoticons into distorted cartoon faces, you can set up text replacements to convert [colon, end parenthesis] to [colon, unicode zero-width space, end parenthesis].

  • Cooking/baking

    • Lots of recipes call for an egg wash that uses an egg and a bit of water or milk, but only require 20% of the resulting mix. You can recycle this into a mini omelette or something if you like, but an alternative is to separate out the white into a separate container, and only use the yolk. (You can add whites separated in this way to a container in the freezer, and use them when you need a bunch for meringue or something.
